Closing the Loop: Bi-Directional BCI

HMM Probabilistic Fusion ROS C++

The Scientific Challenge

Motor Imagery (MI) signals are notoriously noisy and non-stationary. Traditional decoders often ignore the fact that the robot is operating in a structured environment where only certain actions are meaningful at any given time.

Multisensory Fusion Approach

We implemented a Hidden Markov Model (HMM) framework that fuses:

  • Neural Evidence: Real-time EEG features from motor areas.
  • Environmental Evidence: Objects and affordances detected via LiDAR/Vision.
  • Behavioral Constraints: Probabilistic transition maps of the intended task.

Results

By contextualizing the brain signals, we achieved a significant increase in decoding accuracy and a reduction in the "false positive" commands, leading to smoother and more natural human-robot interaction in complex indoor scenarios.
